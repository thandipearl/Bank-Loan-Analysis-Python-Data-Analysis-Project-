# Bank Loan Analysis (Python Data Analysis Project)

## Overview

This project is an end-to-end **data analysis case study** in the finance domain, focused on **bank loan data**. The goal was to explore lending patterns, evaluate risk and uncover insights that can help a bank make better financial decisions.

Using Python, I analyzed a real-world-style dataset to understand:\
-How loans are distributed\
-How customers repay loans\
-What factors influence risk and profitability

---

## Objectives

Analyze overall loan performance using key metrics (KPIs)\
Compare **loan disbursement vs repayment**
Identify **good loans vs bad loans**
Understand customer behavior based on:\
-Income\
-Employment length\
-Home ownership\
-Explore trends across time, regions, and loan purposes

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains approximately **38,000+ loan records** with 24 features, including:

-Loan amount\
-Interest rate\
-Loan status (Fully Paid, Current, Charged Off)\
-Customer income\
-Employment length\
-Loan purpose\
-State (region)\
-Home ownership

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Lending is Profitable

The total **amount received is higher than the amount funded**, showing that the bank earns profit through interest on loans.

---

### 2. Strong Loan Demand

With over **38,000 applications**, the data reflects a high demand for loans, indicating an active and large customer base.

---

### 3. Healthy Cash Flow

For the most recent month:\

-Repayments exceeded new loans issued\
-This suggests a **positive cash flow cycle**.

---

### 4. Balanced Interest Rates

The average interest rate is around **12%**, which is:\

-Competitive for customers\
-Still profitable for the bank

---

### 5. Low Risk Borrowers

The average **Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio (~13%)** suggests that most customers are financially stable and capable of repaying loans.

---

### 6. Good Loans vs Bad Loans

* **Good loans**: Paid on time → generate profit
* **Bad loans**: Defaulted or delayed → cause losses

This is the most critical factor for the bank’s success.

---

### 7. Regional Patterns

Loan activity varies by state, which can help the bank:\
-Focus on high-performing regions\
-Adjust risk strategies geographically

---

### 8. Home Ownership Matters

Customers who own homes tend to be more reliable, indicating **lower default risk**.

---

### 9. Employment Stability

Customers with longer employment history are more likely to:\
-Get approved\
-Repay loans consistently

---

### 10. Loan Purpose Trends

-Loans are taken for various reasons (e.g., car, medical, business), and some categories show higher demand.\
-This can guide targeted loan offerings.
